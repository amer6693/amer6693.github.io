Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)



Early Stages: Thinking about getting on the Sandbox?

What is the Sandbox & is it right for you?

The Regulatory Sandbox Framework (Sandbox) is introduced to encourage innovation and the delivery of financial services by granting regulatory flexibilities for fintech solutions with genuine value proposition to be experimented in a production or live environment. Such flexibilities will be accompanied by appropriate safeguards to preserve financial stability and fair treatment to consumers.

Read more about the Sandbox here

Am I eligible to apply to the Sandbox?

Applicants who are interested to participate in the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox should demonstrate eligibility in accordance with the criteria specified under Section 5 of the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox Framework.

A simplified guidance is provided here
Is the Sandbox only open to Malaysian companies?

The Sandbox is open to all fintech companies including those without any existing presence in Malaysia, but are interested to offer their services in the Malaysian market.

How many applicants will be allowed to participate in the Regulatory Sandbox?

There is no strict limit to the number of participants in Regulatory Sandbox, the Bank will consider the number of participants that can be effectively supported at any given time.

Is it necessary for a fintech company to partner with a financial institution (FI) to be eligible for the Sandbox?

No, the Sandbox is also open to all fintech companies. However, fintech companies that collaborate with FIs could gain added advantages from guidance and support provided by a partner FI.

How do I contact the Sandbox team?

The Sandbox team can be contacted via email, fintech@bnm.gov.my


Pre-application: What do you need to get started?

What can you expect after submitting an application?

  • You should expect to receive an acknowledgment of your application within 1-3 working days.
  • Your application will be assessed against the eligibility criteria specified under section 5 of the Regulatory Sandbox Framework. This would typically involve several discussions to better understand your proposed business model.
  • A decision on your application will be provided within 15 working days from the date at which complete information is submitted to the Bank.
  • The pace at which you progress through the application stage is dependent on the completeness of information you provide and your readiness to test the solution in a live environment.

What do you need to do before you apply?

You should refer to the application guidance here for further information.

How can you apply to the Sandbox?

You can download the application form and email it to fintech@bnm.gov.my

Before you submit your application, you should ensure that you have read and understood the application guidance here in detail.


Application: What happens when you apply to the Sandbox?

What will we assess?

Your application will be assessed against the eligibility criteria specified under section 5 of the Regulatory Sandbox Framework. This would typically involve several discussions to better understand your proposed business model. You should refer to the application guidance here for further information.

What happens if your application is not approved?

When an application is rejected, you should expect a correspondence detailing reasons for the rejection. You can also reach out to the Sandbox team if you need any further clarification on our response.

Will the information submitted to BNM be treated confidentially?

Yes, all information submitted to BNM for the purpose of the Regulatory Sandbox will be treated confidentially.


Testing: How can you build out your FinTech solution with confidence?

What do we expect during testing?

  • During the live test period, applicants are expected to provide regular reports on the progress of the test. Applicants should also prepare for their next steps after the end of the test.
  • Applicants are expected to also prepare for their exit from the Sandbox depending on the success or failure of the test.

What is the maximum time allowed for a testing in Regulatory Sandbox?

  • The initial testing period provided is subject to a maximum of 12 months. Extensions to the testing period may be considered and will be assessed on a case-by-case basis.
  • To extend the testing period, a written application must be submitted by the participants to the Bank no later than 30 calendar days before the expiry of the testing period. The application should state the additional time required and clearly explain reasons for requiring the extension.

What if there are problems during testing?

  • If an applicant experiences significant issues during testing and needs to stop its operations, the Sandbox team should be informed as soon as possible.
  • The applicant will have to ensure an orderly exit from the Sandbox and minimise impact on customers.

What will happen in the event of a failed test?

Applicant who have not managed to achieved the KPIs of a test will need to assess reasons for the failure and determine its next appropriate step, which may include winding down operations in an orderly manner, or considering changes to the business model and alternative business arrangements.

Where the testing is successful, can it be deemed as an automatic approval by BNM for the fintech company to provide the product/service/solution?

Approval to test in the Sandbox may be viewed as a ‘learner’s permit’. A full licence or approval is not necessarily guaranteed after the test.

What will happen upon expiry of the testing period?
  • An approval to participate in the sandbox and any regulatory flexibility accorded will automatically expire, unless prior written approval has been sought from the Bank to extend the testing period.
  • Upon completion of the testing, the Bank will decide whether to allow the product, service or solution to be introduced in the market on a wider scale. Where allowed, participating fintech companies intending to carry out regulated businesses will be assessed based on applicable licensing, approval and registration criteria under the FSA, IFSA and MBSA, as the case may be.

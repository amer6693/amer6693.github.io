General Guidance on the Bank's Regulations

The principal role of the Bank is to promote monetary and financial stability that is conducive to the sustainable growth of the Malaysian economy.

In doing so, the Bank is entrusted with several functions including the fostering of a sound, progressive and inclusive financial system. Accordingly, the Bank’s regulations are based on robust principles and are designed to achieve parity, proportionality and neutrality (more about principles)​

Regulatory objectives – why do we regulate?​

AML/CFT

Objective

  • Effective management of money laundering or terrorist financing risks through appropriate mitigation measures
  • Prevent the infiltration and abuse by criminals to the reporting institutions

Market conduct

Objective

  • Promoting fair and equitable market practices
  • Promoting the financial capabilities of consumers

Prudential

Objective

  • Able to withstand adverse economic condition and maintain customer confidence
  • Address moral hazards by mitigating excessive risk-taking behaviour
  • Protection of depositors and policyholders by preventing mismanagement of funds

Key components of prudential regulation

  • Able to withstand adverse economic condition and maintain customer confidence
  • Address moral hazards by mitigating excessive risk-taking behaviour
  • Protection of depositors and policyholders by preventing mismanagement of funds

Technology Risk

Objective

  • Security and consumer’s privacy
  • Ensure business continuity if technology fails

Policy

  • Managing cyber risk from malware attacks, DDoS and hacks.

How do I know if my business is regulated?​​

The information in this page seeks to provide interested applicants with a broad overview of regulations administered by the Bank to carry out its mandate and functions. Applicants are responsible to comprehensively assess the potential legal implications of the services they intend to offer vis-à-vis regulations administered by the Bank or other regulators such as the Securities Commission. It is common practice to engage an appropriate legal consultant to conduct such an assessment where an applicant is not well positioned to do so. Additionally, applicants in need of guidance or broad advice concerning legal and compliance matters may also consider engaging MDEC’s Fintech Booster programme.​

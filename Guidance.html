Pre-application - What should i do before applying to the Sandbox?


Applicants who are interested to participate in the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox are responsible to adequately demonstrate eligibility in accordance with the criteria specified under Section 5 of the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox Framework. As a simple guidance, an applicant should minimally achieve the following:


Clearly identify whether the proposed product or solution is either wholly or partly incompatible with laws, regulations or standards administered by the Bank

Prior to sending in an application, please do assess the following criteria:

  • The Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox is intended as an avenue for the Bank to consider granting regulatory flexibilities to facilitate the growth of innovative financial services with genuine value proposition that may otherwise be impeded from doing so due to regulations. As such, it is a necessary condition that regulatory impediments are identified clearly to be eligible for the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox. Conversely, if a proposed solution is not under the Bank’s regulatory purview or the applicant is able to fully comply with existing regulations, it would be ineligible.

  • Applicants are responsible to comprehensively assess the potential legal implications of the services they intend to offer vis-à-vis regulations administered by the Bank or other regulators such as the Securities Commission (“SC”). It is common practice to engage an appropriate legal consultant to conduct such an assessment where an applicant is not well positioned to do so. Additionally, applicants in need of guidance or broad advice concerning legal and compliance matters may also consider engaging MDEC’s Fintech Booster programme.

Demonstrate that you have a practical business plan and you are in a reasonable state of readiness with sufficient resources to support testing in the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox.

  • The Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox aims to enable innovative solutions to be deployed and tested in a live environment, within specified parameters and timeframes. As such, an applicant is expected to be minimally at the mid-stage of a start-up’s development phase at the time of application to the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox. Typically, this would include:
    • A ready prototype or minimum viable product to demonstrate the proposed solution
    • Reasonably adequate resources (e.g. financial and staff capacity) to support live testing
    • Appropriate risk assessments and risk mitigation plans
    • A realistic business plan to commercialise the tested solution after exit from the Sandbox

  • Applicants who are at the early stage of development (i.e. ideation or conceptual stage) in need of guidance may consider to engage our local fintech community (e.g. FAOM) or agencies such as MDEC, and other accredited start-up accelerator programme providers.

Adequately substantiate that the proposed solution has a genuinely innovative value proposition

  • Applicants should provide strong evidence to demonstrate a solution’s usefulness, functionality, and potential to improve financial services and satisfy market demand gaps, as stipulated in the Bank’s Regulatory Sandbox Framework and application form. Typically, it would entail a strong market gap assessment or research accompanied with a solid underlying understanding of the relevant market landscape and accurate identification of issues currently in the market.

Enhance your understanding of the Sandbox by going through the Regulatory Sandbox Framework

The Regulatory Sandbox Framework

Journey in The Sandbox

Stage 1: Application - How do I apply to the Sandbox, and what response can I expect?

  • Applicants should submit a completed application form with relevant supporting documentation, to fintech@bnm.gov.my
  • You should expect to receive an acknowledgment of your application within 1-3 working days.
  • Your application will be assessed against the eligibility criteria specified under section 5 of the Regulatory Sandbox Framework. This would typically involve several discussions to better understand your proposed business model.
  • A decision on your application will be provided within 15 working days from the date at which complete information is submitted to the Bank.

Stage 2: Preparation - How should I prepare for testing?

  • Eligible applicants who are admitted into the Sandbox will work with the Bank to prepare for live-testing. This will involve detailed discussions on several matters, including:
    • Determining the appropriate testing parameters.
    • Ensuring adequate safeguards and robust risk management are put in place.
    • Setting key performance indicators (KPI) to measure success and failure of the test, including planning for potential next steps after the test

Stage 3: Live testing- What to expect during testing?

  • Eligible applicants who are admitted into the Sandbox will work with the Bank to prepare for live-testing. This will involve detailed discussions on several matters, including:
    • Applicants who have successfully completed preparations will be given approval to test the solution in the live market within a period not exceeding 12 months. Where necessary, exemptions from relevant regulations will be granted throughout the testing period.
    • During the live test period, applicants are expected to provide regular reports on the progress of the test. Applicants should also prepare for their next steps after the end of the test.

Tips and tricks to note when applying to the Sandbox​

Think thoroughly about whether you are ready to test your proposed business model in a live environment

  • Be thorough in your planning and due diligence. Ensure that your business model is practical and realistic. Make sure you have the necessary resources (e.g. financial resources, human capital) to carry out the test. Conduct an appropriate risk assessment and put in place a reasonable risk management framework. Identify the potential regulatory impediments clearly.​

  • Applicants without adequate experience (e.g. team member or advisor with relevant experience) or coaching (e.g. participation in mentorship or accelerator programs) regarding the basics of financial services are often not sufficiently thorough in considering the commercial feasibility and operational details in their proposed business model.​

Be committed and sincere in engaging with us when you apply to the Sandbox

  • Applicants should regard the Bank’s Sandbox team as your partners when discussing your participation and journey in the Sandbox. ​​

  • Withholding information from the Sandbox team will slow down the assessment process and cause unnecessary delays in processing an application. In some cases, the withholding of pertinent information may also suggest doubts on the integrity and credibility of an applicant, which is an important eligibility criterion for joining the Sandbox.​

Write in a factual manner and communicate your key messages clearly in your application.​

  • Avoid using a marketing pitch styled language as they add little value to the assessment of an application. Be factual, accurate and detailed in describing your business model.​ ​​

  • Ideally, should you include a detailed process flow diagram in your application. That will be helpful for the Sandbox team to better understand your business model.​​

  • Describe the key value propositions of your business model from a customer’s perspective.​

Common misconceptions about the Sandbox

Participation in the Sandbox is considered a validation from the Bank to obtain a full license or approval

    Approval to test in the Sandbox may be viewed as a ‘learner’s permit’. A full licence or approval is not necessarily guaranteed after the test.​

The Bank is too rigid in assessing the eligibility of applicants for participation in the Sandbox

    The most common reasons applicants are not eligible for the Sandbox are as follows:​
    • Applicant did not clearly identify regulatory impediments
      • ​
    • Applicant wrongly identified regulatory impediments, such as specifying regulations that are outside the Bank’s purview​
    • Applicant did not demonstrate a clear value proposition. ​
    • Applicant is not at an adequate state of readiness. Some applicants are at an ideation stage and has yet to determine commercial feasibility of their business model​
    • Applicant did not show basic risk management awareness and capability​

The Bank is too slow in providing a response on my eligibility to participate in the Sandbox

    The Bank is committed to providing a response within 15 working days upon receiving complete information from an applicant.​
    Most applicants are not at a sufficient state of readiness to test in the Sandbox at the time of application. Often, information that are required for assessment may not be readily available.
